Melissa M. Young

Co-Founder & Chief Artistic Officer

Melissa M. Young is a distinguished artistic leader and educator whose three-decade career blends visionary creativity, strategic leadership, and a deep commitment to advancing the performing arts. A Honduran American from Santa Ana, California, Melissa trained at The Ailey School and strengthened her executive acumen through Michigan State University‚Äôs Eli Broad College of Business, earning graduate-level certificates in Leadership, Management, and Analytical Skills. She is also an inaugural cohort member of The Ailey Horton Teacher Certification Program, further advancing her expertise in the Horton Technique.

Melissa spent 30 transformative years with Dallas Black Dance Theatre, rising from company dancer to Artistic Director. Under her leadership, she pioneered a virtual programming model that connected audiences across 40+ states and 35+ countries. Her artistic influence has appeared on renowned stages including The Kennedy Center, Lincoln Center Out-of-Doors, City Center, The Joyce Theater, and at two Cultural Olympiads.

A respected educator specializing in the Lester Horton Technique, Melissa has taught nationally and internationally for universities, conservatories, professional companies, and major festivals. She has also led movement-based leadership experiences for corporate and civic organizations. Her service includes advisory and panel roles for the Texas Commission on the Arts, Mid Atlantic Arts Foundation, Dance Data Project¬Æ, and the City of Dallas Office of Arts and Culture.

Today, Melissa serves as Co-Founder and Chief Artistic Officer of Artistic Directors International; as founder of The Presence Whisperer, a leadership brand rooted in presence, clarity, and embodied excellence; and as a leading practitioner collaborating with the Serendipity Institute for Black Arts and Heritage for the MA in Black British Dance and Performance at the Northern School of Contemporary Dance.
